Pandya Kadungon
Kadungon was a Pandya king of early historic south India.

He is chiefly remembered for reviving the Pandya dynastic power in south India.

Along with the Pallava king Simhavishnu he is credited with ending the Kalabhra rule, marking the beginning of a new era in south India.

Kadungon's title was "Pandyadhiraja", and his capital was Madurai. He was succeeded by his son Maravarman Avanisulamani.
