Srimara Srivallabha
Srimara Srivallabhawas a Pandya king of early medieval south India.

Srimara was famously known as the Parachakra Kolahala ("the Confounder of the Circle of his Enemies").

The Larger Sinnamanur Plates are the major source of information about this Pandya king.

The Pallava version of the events related to this period can be found in the Bahur Plates of Nripatunga
